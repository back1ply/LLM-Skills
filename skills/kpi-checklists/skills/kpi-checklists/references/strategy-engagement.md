# Strategy & Engagement Checklists

## Step 1 — Clear Strategy

### Strategy Fitness Checklist

Before developing any measures, validate your strategy passes these tests:

- [ ] Is the strategy written down?
- [ ] Does the strategy make sense to an intelligent, but non-specialist, reader?
- [ ] Is there broad management consensus that the strategy is correct?
- [ ] Is your strategy relevant to the key decision-makers? (Do any dismiss it when discussed?)
- [ ] Is your strategy linked to clear and specific strategic objectives?
- [ ] Are the strategic objective descriptions physically accessible by all managers?
- [ ] Are the strategic objectives broad and "non-dated" (not just important actions with completion dates)?
- [ ] Are managers across the organisation familiar with the strategic objectives without scrambling for documents?
- [ ] Are key decision-makers either working to, or attempting to work to, the strategy?
- [ ] Is the current strategy still relevant and up to date?
- [ ] Are there fewer than seven strategic objectives?

If any answer is "no", fix the strategy problems before creating measures. No strategy equals no meaningful measures.

### Strategic Objectives Clarity Checklist

- [ ] Is each strategic objective articulated in no more than two sentences?
- [ ] Does the description make sense?
- [ ] Is it possible to interpret a statement in a radically different way? (Test by getting several people to explain what it means — variations can be surprising.)
- [ ] Are there any "woolly words" or "management buzzwords"? Remove or replace "synergy", "excellence", "outstanding", "empowerment" with clear, simple phrases.

### Good Strategic Objective Examples

- Increase bank capital ratio to 11%.
- Reduce loss of life through road accidents by 50%.
- Become the biggest UK supplier of rubber ducks.
- Be the fastest-delivering mail order memory stick retailer.
- Have the widest selection of digital delivery channels in Europe.

### Right Number of Strategic Objectives

- Fewer than 3: probably not breaking strategy down enough
- More than 7: risk confusing people and fragmenting approach
- Sweet spot: 2-7 strategic objectives

## Step 2 — Engage Stakeholders

### RACI Matrix

| Role | Description | Communication |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Responsible** | People who do the work to achieve the task | Direct involvement |
| **Accountable** | Person ultimately answerable for correct completion (signs it off) | Final authority |
| **Consulted** | People whose opinions are sought (subject matter experts) | Two-way dialogue |
| **Informed** | People who need to be kept up to date on progress | One-way updates |

**How to build it**:
1. List roles/names across the top columns
2. List KPIs, reports, or strategic objectives down the side
3. For each intersection ask: Is this person R, A, C, or I for this item?
4. Document and circulate for review

### Stakeholder Types

| Type | Role |
|------|------|
| **Data originators** | At or near the point where data is collected or generated |
| **Data aggregators** | Pull data together, often part of IT function |
| **Data packagers** | Perform analyses, create reports and dashboards (MI/BI team) |
| **Internal customers** | Use the output to make decisions that affect the business |

### Communications Plan — Message, Purpose, and Audience

- [ ] Why are you communicating?
- [ ] What are you trying to change through communications?
- [ ] If you succeed, what would be different?
- [ ] Do you understand what your audience currently thinks? (Survey if not.)
- [ ] Is that difference quantifiable?
- [ ] Who do you need to communicate with? (Use RACI to segment.)
- [ ] Write key messages for each audience segment.

### Communications Plan — Design Activities

- [ ] **Delivery method** — Face-to-face, email, intranet, or teleconference?
- [ ] **Timing** — When will you communicate? How many updates/reminders?
- [ ] **Owner** — Who delivers? Are they trained, suitable, motivated?
- [ ] **Audience** — Completely clear who is included and what type they receive?
- [ ] **Target outcomes** — What should audience know, think, or do as a result?

### Communications Plan — Delivery

- [ ] Who will deliver the message?
- [ ] What preparation do they need?
- [ ] Have you drafted "frequently asked questions and responses"?
- [ ] How many people needed to cover all the target audience?
- [ ] What other resources? (Intranet pages, SharePoint sites, etc.)
- [ ] How can the audience feed back comments and questions?
- [ ] What checks ensure the plan is delivered as intended?
- [ ] How can you tell if the communication has been effective?
- [ ] How will you know if further communication is needed?

### Key Communications Principles

- [ ] Use targeting — don't encourage people to ignore your communications
- [ ] Put yourself in the shoes of your audience
- [ ] Senior endorsement helps people take the message seriously
- [ ] Be as honest as you can be
- [ ] If expecting a bumpy ride, have one-to-ones with key players before group sessions
- [ ] Accept you won't always have the right information — commit to getting an answer and deliver on time
- [ ] Be very familiar with the message

## Stakeholder Interviews

### Semi-Structured Interview Checklist

**Introductions**:
- [ ] About you (interviewer) — who you are, why you are here
- [ ] Your objectives — what you want from the interview
- [ ] Whether outputs are anonymous or not

**About the interviewee**:
- [ ] Their background
- [ ] How long in current role
- [ ] What they want from the interview
- [ ] Any concerns
- [ ] Any questions

**Open questions**:
- [ ] Which reports, measures, and dashboards are they regularly exposed to?
- [ ] Biggest worries about current reporting/dashboarding?
- [ ] Single priority to improve regarding measures and reports?
- [ ] Confidence in data quality in the organisation?
- [ ] Outputs they would like to see that aren't currently produced?

**Closed questions**:
- [ ] Have the company's strategic objectives been communicated to them?
- [ ] Can they recall those objectives?
- [ ] Any specific examples of measurement issues needing urgent fixing?

### Stakeholder Workshop Tips

- Be clear about the purpose of the session
- No more than 10-12 attendees per facilitator
- Be aware of management structures — don't mix outspoken managers with tongue-tied subordinates
- Be clear and honest about confidentiality and re-use of comments
- Note key points and play back at the end for validation

### How to Tell What People Really Think

Show examples from previous projects or other organisations. Listen carefully to:
- What they say they like and don't like about samples
- Initial reactions (these reveal true feelings about current documents)
- This helps avoid going down blind alleys later in the process
